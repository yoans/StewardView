Your access

View access is enough for oversight

Treasurers and admins enter the data. Elders and trustees mainly read and ask good questions. That keeps the books controlled while keeping leadership informed.

  • See the dashboard: bank balances, recent activity, fund totals
  • Browse transactions and monthly reports
  • Compare budget vs. actual by category
  • Open each earmarked fund and see what’s in it

You don’t need to “run” the books — you need a clear window into them.

Categories

Categories answer: “What kind of spending or giving is this?”

Every dollar is tagged to a category so we can see patterns — not just a pile of deposits and checks.

Income examples

Tithes & Offerings · Directed Contributions · Special Events · Interest

Expense examples

Utilities · Missions · Staff · Building · Benevolence · Ministry programs

In meetings: when we discuss “should we spend more on X?”, look at that category’s budget and actuals first.

Earmarked funds

Funds answer: “Is this money already promised to a purpose?”

Categories classify activity. Earmarked funds hold savings and gifts that belong to a specific cause — like a building reserve, missions, youth, or benevolence.

Step 1
Someone gives

Online (Givelify) or otherwise, often toward an envelope or campaign.

Step 2
We earmark

The gift is mapped to the right fund so it isn’t mixed into unrestricted cash.

Step 3
You can see it

Fund balances and history show what we can spend for that purpose.

StewardView

Contributions

Watch how giving is directed

StewardView tracks contributions — including Givelify imports — and shows whether gifts went to the General Fund or to a directed / earmarked fund.

  • General giving supports the operating budget (Tithes & Offerings)
  • Directed giving is set aside for a named purpose (Directed Contributions → fund)
  • Pending earmarks mean a gift still needs to be assigned to a fund

This is how we honor donor intent and avoid spending restricted gifts on something else.

Budget oversight

Budget vs. Actual is your monthly scoreboard

Leadership builds the plan. StewardView shows how we’re tracking — by category, month, and year-to-date.

Ask here

“Are we over in utilities?” “Is missions giving where we hoped?” “Can the building fund cover this repair?”

Decide with facts

Approve spending against available budget and available earmarked balances — not guesswork.

How you’ll use it

A simple rhythm for elders & trustees

  • Before a meeting — open Budget and Funds; note anything that looks off
  • During discussion — pull up the category or earmarked fund on screen
  • When approving spend — confirm: budget room + correct fund (if restricted)
  • Afterward — leave the bookkeeping to treasurer/admin; keep asking for clarity

View access = informed voice. You steward decisions; the system holds the detail.
